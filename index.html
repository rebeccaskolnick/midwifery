Who is a midwife?

Midwife means "with woman"

A midwife is a healthcare professional who provides a variety of services for women that can include gynecological examinations, contraceptive counseling, family planning and preconception care, prenatal care, labor and delivery care, newborn care, and menopausal management.

The expertise in providing individualized care throughout conception, gestation, labor, delivery, and after birth is a distinguishing factor of midwifery from other maternal health care professions.

Model of care

The midwives model of care is based on the simple belief that pregnancy and birth are natural life processes. A midwife does everything in his or her power to minimize and eliminate all unnecessary interventions.

The midwifery model of care includes: Monitoring the physical, psychological, and social well-being of the mother throughout the childbearing cycle, providing the mother with individualized education, counseling, and prenatal care, continuous hands-on assistance during labor and delivery, and postpartum support, minimizing technological interventions, along with identifying and referring women who require obstetrical attention.

Why choose a midwife?

The central axis of midwifery is the human-to-human connection. The emotional, practical, and social support provided by a midwife is unlike that of any other healthcare professional.

A midwife becomes an integral part of each family system he or she helps facilitate the creation of.

In the United States, there are five main classifications of midwives based on training and practice methods.

Certified Nurse-Midwife (CNM)

An individual trained and licensed in both nursing and midwifery. Nurse-midwives possess at least a bachelor’s degree from an accredited institution of higher education and are certified by the American College of Nurse Midwives.

Certified Professional Midwife (CPM)

An individual trained in midwifery who meets practice standards of the North American Registry of Midwives.

Direct-Entry Midwife (DEM)

an independent individual trained in midwifery through a variety of sources that can include: self-study, apprenticeship, a midwifery school, or a college/university program.

Certified Midwife (CM)

an individual trained and certified in midwifery. Certified midwives possess at least a bachelor’s degree from an accredited institution of higher education and are certified by the American College of Nurse Midwives.

Lay Midwife

an individual who is not certified or licensed as a midwife but has been trained informally through self-study or apprenticeship.
